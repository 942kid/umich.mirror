Summary of the Data

We subset from the OPTN/UNOS data (as of Sept. 2015) the cohort listed between Jan. 01, 2008 and Dec. 31, 2013 for one-year graft/patient survival analysis. There are in total 94856 ESRD patients (censoring rates for graft failure is 81 and censoring rates for patient death is 87.8; out of which 815 had more than one transplants) and 95687 kidney transplants from 247 transplant centers included in the analysis. For convenience, in this prelminary analysis, multiple transplants from the same patients are treated as indepenent observations.

KDPI is categorized (KDPIC) as 0-10%, 10-90%, 90+%, Missing or Living Donor (NOTE: since only deceased donor will have KDPI, we include donor type as another category in this variable; there is a recent published paper on LKDPI), we did not include other donor characteristics from last time (age, gender, BMI etc.).

Diabetes Types as a Risk Factor

The DIAB in the data are coded as follows:

  • 1: No diabetes;
  • 2: Type I diabetes;
  • 3: Type II diabetes;
  • 4: Unknown Type;
  • 5: Missing and Not Reported.

We then fit a stratified Cox model including all the risk factor and using TX centers as the strata:

  • Recipient: Age, race, sex, BMI, sensitization status (using cPRA), diabetes, peripheral vascular disease
  • Donor: KDPIC
  • Transplantation: cold ischemia time, HLA mismatch ( 0-2,3-4, 5-6)

Separate models are fitted for graft (T.GRF) and patient (T.PT).

Time-Varying Coefficient for Diabetes Types

In this section, we only use the subset with non-missing diabetes (N = 94827). We first check the effect of diabetes on the graft failure rate and mortality rate by the time period after TX (3 mo, 3-12 mo), and plot them in the following bar-plots. These rates are calculated as \[ \frac{\text{Number of failures}}{\text{Number of patients at-risk} \times \text{Length of the time-period}}. \] The second row of bar-plots shows the relative rates with the No Diab group as the reference.

We fit a Cox model with varying effects for diabetes types (Type I, II vs.No). The cutting point for the time-varying coefficients are 3 months and 12 months (tint is in 0-3 months, 3-12 months or 12 + months).

Interaction between Dialysis Time and Diabetes

We first check the cross table between diabetes status (Yes or No) and the dialysis time (Missing, Pre-emptive, 0-2 years, 2 years+). The dialysis time is calculated by substracting dialysis date (either DIAL_DATE or DIALYSIS_DATE) from TX_DATE, which is decided as follows:

  • both dialysis dates are non-missing and equal; use either one;
  • both dialysis dates are non-missing and unequal, but one is after TX_DATE; use the one precedes TX_DATE;
  • both dialysis dates are non-missing and unequal, and both precede TX_DATE; use DIALYSIS_DATE;
  • only one of DIAL_DATE and DIALYSIS_DATE is missing; use the non-missing dialysis date in the calculation;
  • Preemptive: both DIAL_DATE and DIALYSIS_DATE are missing and DIAL_TRR == "N";
  • Miss: both DIAL_DATE and DIALYSIS_DATE are missing and DIAL_TRR != "N".
