About Me

I was born and raised in Shanxi, China. My Chinese name (毋凡) means "non-trivial" in mathematics or "non-normal" in statistics; whereas my English/Pinyin name (Fan) usually shows up on the air conditioner control panel.

I am currently a Ph.D. Candidate in biostatistics at U of Michigan. My dissertation focus on efficiency improvement in regression methods for left-truncated survival data and clustering methods for multivariate sparse and irregular functional/longitudinal data.

My research interest covers semi-parametric models, outcome dependent sampling, longitudinal data and cluster analysis. I am also interested in statistical learning, Bayesian inferences, causal inferences and data visualization.

Beside statistics, I enjoy reading, rock 'n' roll music, web comics (e.g. xkcd and SMBC), mentoring dogs and cats, discovering stuff in thrift stores and wandering around without specific purposes.
